IDA

Interior Design Assistant — a research platform for AI-assisted personalization of interior concepts

PRESS RELEASE
June 2026
Szczecin, Poland

A doctoral researcher at the Academy of Art in Szczecin, Poland, launches early access to IDA — an AI tool that generates personalized interior concepts from a room photo and a user preference profile, instead of preset style templates.

What is IDA

IDA (Interior Design Assistant) is an experimental research platform developed as part of a PhD dissertation. It combines environmental psychology, Research Through Design methodology, and generative AI models. Users upload a photo of their space and complete aesthetic preference profiling — explicit (questionnaires, semantic scales) and behavioral (visual choices, response times). In full mode, an optional Big Five questionnaire (IPIP-NEO-120) is available. The system synthesizes the profile and generates several personalized visual interior concepts aligned with the user, not with a social media trend.

Key differentiators

  • Personalization via user profile, not “Scandinavian / loft / boho” presets
  • Quick mode (~3–5 min) or full mode with a deeper psycho-aesthetic profile
  • Academic context: PhD at Academy of Art in Szczecin; supervisor: Prof. Andreas Guskos
  • Stack: Next.js, Vertex AI, Google Cloud (Cloud Run, Cloud Storage)
  • Research data collected in natural product-use context

Who it is for

IDA targets people planning a renovation or redecoration who want to see an aesthetic direction for their space before making costly purchase decisions. The tool supports the inspiration and concept-comparison stage — especially when users cannot name their preferred style.

What IDA does not do

The platform produces visual concepts and inspirational material. It does not replace an interior designer or architect, does not produce construction documentation, and does not account for building codes, installations, or formal approvals. Results should be treated as a starting point for further design work.

Participation requires informed consent for research data processing under the privacy policy (project-ida.com/privacy). The project is supported by Google Cloud Research Credits, Google for Startups Cloud Program, Modal for Startups, and Vercel.
